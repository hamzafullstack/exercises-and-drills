JavaScript Operators | Practice Exercises

Based on the Basic Operators, Maths chapter from JavaScript.info

Exercise 1: Terminology Drill

Identify the operands and the operator in each expression. Then classify whether the operator is unary or binary.

  • Example 1: In 5 * 2, which part is the operator? Which are operands?
  • Example 2: In -x, is the minus unary or binary? Why?

Exercise 2: Arithmetic Operators Mastery

Pick two numbers (e.g., 7 and 3). Predict the results of each operation:

  • Addition +
  • Subtraction -
  • Multiplication *
  • Division /
  • Remainder %
  • Exponentiation **

Reflect on how % and ** behave differently from the other operators.

Exercise 3: Remainder Operator Challenges

For each pair of numbers (a, b), determine the result of a % b and explain what it means.

  • (5, 2)
  • (8, 3)
  • (8, 4)
  • (10, 6)
  • (15, 7)

Ask yourself: What happens when the dividend is smaller than the divisor?

Exercise 4: Exponentiation Insights

Work out these examples and notice the patterns:

  • 2 ** 2
  • 2 ** 3
  • 2 ** 4
  • 9 ** 0.5

Reflect: What happens when the exponent is 0.5? What about other non-integers?

Exercise 5: Mixed Operators & Precedence

Without calculating first, decide the order of operations. Then compute:

  • 1 + 2 * 3
  • (1 + 2) * 3
  • 9 / 2 * 3
  • 9 / (2 * 3)
  • 1 + 2 ** 3 / 2 - 1

Exercise 6: Explore Operator Overlaps

Choose a symbol that works both as unary and binary (like -).

  • Example of unary usage: -x
  • Example of binary usage: 5 - 2

Explain the difference clearly.

  • Terminology makes you precise and avoids confusion.
